Noble ranks among the top hospitality firms in the U.S. for 12th consecutive year.

2009

Noble receives Marriott's Partnership Circle Award – Marriott's highest honor.

2008

INC 500 ranks Noble among the top 500 Private Companies in the United States.

2007

Noble closes its third Hospitality Real Estate Fund with $310 million in equity commitments.

Noble helps launch Hyatt Hotel’s franchise platform and is honored with Hyatt’s North America Strategic Partnership Award.

2006

Noble becomes a franchisee of Starwood Hotels and Resorts.

2005

Noble becomes a franchisee of Starbucks Coffee.

2003

Noble establishes public-private investment platform through the acquisition of Stormont Development Group.

Noble closes its second Hospitality Real Estate Fund with $182 million in equity commitments.

2001

Noble acquires the Franklin Marriott Cool Springs which goes on to win several Marriott and Noble awards for quality and performance.

2000

Noble reaches $100 million in investments and 1,000 rooms in the same year.

1999

Noble closes its first Hospitality Real Estate Fund with $40 million in equity commitments.

Noble partners with America’s Promise to provide educational opportunities to underprivileged children.

1997

Noble opens its 10th hotel. The Homewood Suites Atlana Buckhead - representing the 50th Homewood Suites in the system.

1996

Noble becomes a franchisee of Marriott International.

1993

Noble opens its first hotel: the Hampton Inn in Peachtree City, Georgia and becomes one of the first franchisees of Promus Hotels which later becomes acquired by Hilton.

Noble Investment Group is founded in Atlanta, GA.
